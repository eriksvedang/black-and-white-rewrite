Black & White - unoffical (clarified) rules

The board game Black & White by Krzysztof Matusik is a very cool and well-designed war game, oozing with style. Unfortunately the rules are a bit fuzzy at times, so this document tries to be a source of truth for those wanting to play the game in the most clear (and hopefully enjoyable!) way possible. In the situations where the official rule book is ambiguos I've primarily tried to get the correct ruling from the BoardGameGeek forums for the game. When there hasn't been an official answer, I've tried to come up with my own ruling that is as fun and close to the "spirit of the game" as possible. Please note that this is just my interpretation of the rules and not official in any way. I've also taken the liberty to re-arrange the order of some of the rules a little bit. Enjoy!

Setup

  1. Place the game board in the middle of the table.
  2. Choose sides, one player is white () and the other is black ().
  3. Agree on a scenario card to use, see below for more details on the various kinds of scenarios.
  4. Add hexagon tiles to the game board according to the scenario card.
  5. Give each player a number of special tokens depending on what kind of scenario you're playing.
  6. Place the screen in the middle of the board.
  7. Select units to a total maximum cost of 20 and place them on the board (facing towards yourself). The scenario card shows your front line, no unit can start closer to the enemy than that.
  8. Remove the screen from the middle of the board.
  9. Randomly decide who goes first.

Note

  • Unit cards are kept hidden until that unit reveals itself during a fight or an activation.
